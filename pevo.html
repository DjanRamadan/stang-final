First Generation (1965-1973)

1965 Mustang

The original pony car, available as a coupe, fastback, or convertible. It featured a variety of engines, from a 170 cubic inch (2.8L) straight-6 to a 289 cubic inch (4.7L) V8 producing up to 271 horsepower.

1967 Mustang GT

Known for its more aggressive styling and improved performance options, including the 390 cubic inch (6.4L) V8 engine producing 320 horsepower.

1969 Boss 429

High-performance model designed for racing. The Boss 429 had a 429 cubic inch (7.0L) V8 producing 375 horsepower.

Second Generation (1974-1978)

Mustang II Ghia

Focused on luxury and comfort, with a more refined interior and a 2.8L V6 engine producing 105 horsepower as an option.

Mustang II Cobra II

A sportier version introduced in 1976, featuring a distinctive appearance package with racing stripes and a more powerful 302 cubic inch (5.0L) V8 engine producing 140 horsepower.

Third Generation (1979-1993)

1982 Mustang GT

Marked the return of the GT badge with a new 5.0L V8 engine producing 157 horsepower, offering improved performance and handling.

1984 Mustang SVO

A high-performance model with a turbocharged 2.3L inline-4 engine producing 175-205 horsepower, advanced suspension, and unique styling cues.

1993 Mustang Cobra

The final performance model of the Fox platform, featuring a 5.0L V8 engine tuned by Ford's Special Vehicle Team (SVT) to produce 235 horsepower.

Fourth Generation (1994-2004)

1994 Mustang GT

Featured a new design and a 5.0L V8 engine producing 215 horsepower, later replaced by the more advanced 4.6L V8 producing up to 305 horsepower in the Mach 1.

2000 SVT Cobra R

A limited-edition, race-ready model with a 5.4L V8 engine producing 385 horsepower, lightweight components, and enhanced aerodynamics.

2003 Mustang Mach 1

A nostalgic performance model with retro styling cues, a shaker hood scoop, and a 4.6L V8 engine delivering 305 horsepower.

Fifth Generation (2005-2014)

2005 Mustang GT

Introduced with a retro design inspired by the original Mustang and powered by a 4.6L V8 engine producing 300 horsepower.

2008 Mustang Bullitt

A special edition inspired by the 1968 film "Bullitt," featuring unique styling and a 315-horsepower 4.6L V8 engine.

2011 Mustang Boss 302

Revived the legendary Boss 302 name with a 5.0L V8 engine producing 444 horsepower and track-focused performance enhancements.

2013 Shelby GT500

Powered by a 5.8L supercharged V8 engine delivering a staggering 662 horsepower, making it one of the most powerful production Mustangs ever.

Sixth Generation (2015-2023)

2015 Mustang GT

Introduced with a new platform, independent rear suspension, and a 5.0L V8 engine producing 435 horsepower.

2016 Shelby GT350

A high-performance model featuring a 5.2L flat-plane crank V8 engine producing 526 horsepower, designed for track use with advanced aerodynamics and suspension.

2020 Mustang Shelby GT500

Powered by a 5.2L supercharged V8 engine producing 760 horsepower, featuring advanced performance technologies and a dual-clutch transmission.

2021 Mustang Mach 1

A modern take on the classic Mach 1, featuring a 5.0L V8 engine with 480 horsepower and performance enhancements from the GT350 and GT500.

Seventh Generation (2024-Present)

2024 Mustang GT

Features updated styling, advanced aerodynamics, and an enhanced 5.0L Coyote V8 engine with increased power and efficiency, producing around 480 horsepower.

2024 Mustang Dark Horse

A new performance-oriented model designed for track enthusiasts, featuring unique styling elements, advanced suspension, and a high-performance version of the 5.0L V8 engine producing 500 horsepower.
